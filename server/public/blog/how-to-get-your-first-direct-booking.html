How to get your first direct booking within 30 days

NB
The NestBook Team
April 2026
· 5 min read

You've set up your property. You've taken the photos. You've listed on Airbnb and Booking.com and the first few bookings have trickled in. But every time you see that confirmation email, there's a small sting — the commission line. Direct bookings are the goal. Here's how to get your first one within 30 days.

Week 1 — Get your booking widget live

The single most important thing you can do is make it possible for guests to book directly through your own website. Not "email me and we'll arrange something." A proper booking form, available 24 hours a day, that confirms instantly.

If you don't have a website, a simple one-page site with your photos, a description and a booking widget is enough to start. Guests don't need a complex website. They need to know what they're booking and how to book it.

Once your widget is live, put the link everywhere — your email signature, your social media profiles, your Google Business listing. Every touchpoint should lead back to your direct booking page.

Week 2 — Claim and optimise your Google Business profile

If someone searches for your property name, what comes up? For most small properties the answer is: not much.

A Google Business profile costs nothing and takes an afternoon to set up. Add your photos, your description, your opening hours and — crucially — a link to your direct booking page. When past guests leave reviews, respond to every one. Google rewards engagement.

Properties with complete, active Google Business profiles appear higher in local search results. A guest searching "gîte Dordogne" or "B&B Lake District" is exactly the guest you want. Make sure they can find you.

Week 3 — Email your past guests

If you've hosted even a handful of guests, you have something valuable: their email addresses.

A simple, personal email — not a newsletter, not a marketing campaign, just a message from you — can generate bookings surprisingly quickly. Tell them you now accept direct bookings through your website, that there's no booking platform fee involved, and that you'd love to welcome them back.

Most guests who had a good stay are happy to book direct if they know it's possible. Many assume they have to go back through Airbnb or Booking.com because they don't know another way exists.

Week 4 — Share on social media, personally

A post from your personal Facebook or Instagram account saying "we now take direct bookings — here's the link" will reach people who know you and trust you. Friends of friends. Local connections. People who've been thinking about a holiday and just needed a nudge.

This isn't about building a following or running ads. It's about telling the people already in your network that you're open for business and bookings are easy.

The direct booking mindset

Direct bookings don't replace platforms overnight. Most experienced property owners use a mix — platforms for visibility and new guests, direct bookings for returning guests and personal referrals.

But every direct booking is a relationship built entirely between you and your guest. No algorithm. No commission. No platform that can change its rules overnight and affect your income.

Your first direct booking is the hardest. After that, it gets easier.
