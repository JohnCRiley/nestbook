5 things French gîte owners wish they'd known before their first season

NB
The NestBook Team
April 2026
· 5 min read

Every gîte owner remembers their first season. The excitement of the first booking. The panic of the second. The slow realisation that running a holiday property is a proper business — one that requires systems, organisation and a surprising amount of patience.

We've talked to dozens of gîte owners across France. Here are the five things that come up again and again.

1 Double bookings are more common than you think

It starts innocently. A booking comes in through Airbnb. Another through your own website. A third from a friend of a friend who called and you jotted down in the diary. By July you have two families arriving on the same Saturday for the same property.

The paper diary and the online platform don't talk to each other. Neither does your memory. The result is one of the most stressful conversations a gîte owner ever has.

The fix is a single source of truth — one system where every booking lives, regardless of where it came from. When a date is taken, it's taken everywhere.

2 Guests expect instant confirmation

The days of "I'll send you a confirmation letter in the post" are over. Today's guests book on their phone at 11pm and expect a confirmation email in their inbox before they go to sleep.

If you're manually sending confirmation emails the next morning, you're already behind. Worse, the guest might not be sure the booking actually went through — and might book somewhere else in the meantime.

Automated booking confirmations aren't a luxury. For today's guests, they're the baseline.

3 The quiet months are when you should be doing admin

November to February feels like downtime. It isn't. It's when your reviews catch up with you, when your calendar needs updating for next year, when your pricing needs reviewing, when your website needs refreshing.

The owners who thrive in year two are the ones who used the quiet months in year one to build proper systems. The ones who didn't spend October to March on the sofa are the ones who hit June organised and ready.

4 Direct bookings are worth far more than platform bookings

Airbnb and Booking.com are brilliant for getting started. They bring you guests you'd never have found yourself. But they take 15–20% of every booking in commission.

On an €800 week that's €120–160 gone before you've changed a single bedsheet.

A guest who books directly through your own website costs you nothing in commission. Over a season of 20 bookings the difference can be thousands of euros. A simple booking form on your own website, linked to your availability calendar, pays for itself many times over.

5 Your guests remember how you made them feel, not what you gave them

The welcome basket, the local wine, the handwritten note — these matter enormously. But what guests remember most is how easy everything was. How quickly you replied to their question. How clear the check-in instructions were. How smoothly the booking process worked from start to finish.

The operational stuff — the booking system, the confirmations, the calendar — isn't just admin. It's hospitality. When it works invisibly, guests feel looked after. When it doesn't, they notice.

The best gîte owners understand that great hospitality starts before the guests arrive.
