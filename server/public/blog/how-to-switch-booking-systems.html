How to switch booking systems without losing your data

NB
The NestBook Team
May 2026
· 4 min read

One of the most common reasons property owners stay with booking software they're not happy with is the fear of losing their data. Guest records built up over years. Booking history. Room configurations. It's a legitimate concern. But switching is usually less painful than it seems.

What data actually matters

Before you panic about switching, it's worth identifying what you'd actually lose and what you wouldn't.

Your booking history — past stays, guest names, amounts paid — is useful for your accountant but you don't need it in your new system. A simple export from your current platform, saved as a spreadsheet, is sufficient for records purposes.

Your future bookings — anything confirmed for the next few months — genuinely need to be carried across. Most systems let you export these or at minimum view them so you can re-enter the important ones.

Your guest contact list — emails and phone numbers — is worth exporting carefully. Under GDPR you're entitled to this data and any reputable platform will provide it.

The practical steps

Step 1 — Export everything before you cancel

Log into your current system and find the export function. Download your guest list, your booking history and any financial reports you might need. Save them somewhere safe.

Step 2 — Set up your new system in parallel

Don't cancel anything yet. Set up NestBook — or whichever system you're moving to — alongside your existing one. Add your rooms, your pricing, your settings. Get familiar with it before you commit.

Step 3 — Enter your upcoming bookings

Go through your confirmed future bookings and enter them into the new system. For most small properties this takes an hour at most.

Step 4 — Update your booking widget

If you have a booking button on your website, swap the old embed code for the new one. This takes about five minutes.

Step 5 — Update your iCal links

If you've connected your calendar to Booking.com or Airbnb via iCal, update those links to your new system's URLs. In NestBook, find the updated links in Settings under Calendar Sync.

Step 6 — Cancel your old system

Once you're confident everything is running correctly in the new system, cancel the old one. Most platforms require notice — check your contract.

How long does it take?

For a small property with a straightforward setup, switching booking systems typically takes an afternoon. The fear is usually worse than the reality.

The right time to switch is at a quiet point in your calendar — January or February for most UK properties, after the Christmas guests have left and before the spring bookings pick up.

A word on your guest data

Import your guest email list into NestBook's guest records so returning guests are already in the system when they call. A familiar face at check-in — greeted by name, with their preferences noted — is one of the things small independent properties do better than any chain hotel.

Don't lose that advantage in the switch.
