Why gîte and holiday cottage owners need a direct booking system

NB
The NestBook Team
June 2026
· 7 min read
This blog is written in English. For other languages, your browser's built-in translation tool works well — right-click anywhere on the page and select "Translate".

Gîtes and holiday cottages are different to hotels and B&Bs. Guests rent the whole property — not a room. They often stay a week, not a night. And every time a booking comes through Airbnb or Booking.com, you're handing over 15–20% of your income before you've even unlocked the door.

A dedicated booking system built for self-catering properties changes that equation. Here's what the numbers look like — and what to look for when choosing one.

1 The commission problem for self-catering owners

Platforms are brilliant for visibility, especially in your first season. But the maths of commission compounds painfully once you're established.

£1,500 20 weeks £4,500 £6,000

That's money that could be yours — if even half of your bookings came direct. A gîte booking software that costs £19/month adds up to £228/year. You recoup that cost with a single direct booking.

Every direct booking that replaces an Airbnb booking saves you £120–300 in commission. For most self-catering owners, one season of direct bookings more than covers the cost of software for years to come.

2 Why whole-property rentals are different to B&Bs

Most booking software was built for hotels and B&Bs — places where guests book individual rooms and multiple parties occupy the property simultaneously. For a gîte or holiday cottage, that model doesn't fit.

When you rent a whole property:

  • There's only one booking per stay — the entire property
  • Guests typically arrive on a fixed changeover day (Friday or Saturday)
  • Minimum stays are usually a week rather than a night
  • Pricing is often per property, not per person
  • The availability calendar is property-wide — a booking blocks everything

Software built around room-based bookings forces self-catering owners to awkwardly model their property as a single "room" and fight the system at every turn. You need software that understands whole-property rental from the ground up.

3 What a direct booking system actually gives you

A good self-catering booking system does several things a spreadsheet and an Airbnb listing can't:

  • A single availability calendar that stays in sync across every channel — so you never double-book
  • Automatic booking confirmations sent the moment a guest books, without you lifting a finger
  • A guest record for every person who has ever stayed — useful for repeat business and targeted offers
  • Revenue reports that show you how this season compares to last, where your bookings come from, and which weeks are most profitable
  • A booking widget you can drop onto your own website — so guests can check availability and book direct, without leaving your site

Taken together, these features replace a stack of admin that currently lives in your head, your inbox and your paper diary.

When you set your property to whole-property mode, the booking experience changes accordingly:

  • Guests see a single rate and a single "Book this property" button

It's the difference between a system that was retrofitted for self-catering and one where self-catering is a first-class use case.

6 iCal sync with Booking.com and Airbnb

You don't have to choose between platforms and direct bookings. iCal sync lets you keep your Airbnb and Booking.com listings active while feeding all those bookings back into NestBook in real time.

When an Airbnb booking comes in, it automatically blocks those dates in your NestBook calendar — preventing any direct booking for the same period. And when a direct booking comes in through your NestBook widget, it feeds back to your Airbnb calendar via iCal, preventing a double-booking from the platform side.

The result: a single source of truth, whether the booking came from a platform or direct. No more cross-referencing two calendars. No more double bookings.
