Getting started

Sign up, set up your property, and go live in minutes

Sign up and verify your email

  1. Go to nestbook.io/app/register and fill in your name, email and a password.
  2. Check your email inbox for a verification link from NestBook and click it.
  3. You'll be taken directly into your dashboard, ready to set up your property.

If you don't receive the verification email within a few minutes, check your spam folder or email hello@nestbook.io.

Add your property details

Go to Settings in the left sidebar and fill in your property name, address, check-in and check-out times, and your preferred currency. Click Save changes when done.

Choose your property type

NestBook supports a wide range of accommodation types. Select the one that best describes your property:

  • B&B / Bed & Breakfast — individual rooms with breakfast included
  • Gîte / Holiday cottage — self-catering whole-property rental
  • Guesthouse — multiple rooms, shared common areas
  • Lodge / Villa / Farmhouse / Château — whole-property rentals
  • Glamping / Narrowboat — unique or alternative accommodation

Choose your rental type

NestBook supports two rental modes:

  • Individual rooms (IP mode) — guests book one or more rooms. Best for B&Bs, guesthouses and hotels. The calendar shows each room separately.
  • Whole property (WP mode) — guests book the entire property for their stay. Best for gîtes, holiday cottages, lodges and villas. The calendar shows one property per month view.
Rental type is set when you create your property and cannot be changed after rooms are added. Contact support if you need to switch modes.

Set your language and currency

In Settings → Property details, choose your display currency (e.g. GBP, EUR) and the default language for guest-facing emails and booking confirmations. Guests on your booking page can switch to any of the 5 supported languages (EN, FR, DE, ES, NL).

Choose your colour theme

In Settings → Booking page, choose a colour accent for your public booking page — green, blue, amber, or rose. This affects the header, buttons, and highlight colours guests see.

Your free property webpage

Your public booking page at nestbook.io/book/your-name

What your booking page looks like

Every NestBook property gets a public booking page at nestbook.io/book/your-slug. Guests can see your property photos, room descriptions, availability and prices — and book directly with you. The page works in 5 languages and on any device.

See a live example at nestbook.io/book/domaine-des-lavandes (demo property).

Editing your booking page URL (slug)

Go to Settings → Booking page and change the URL slug field. Your slug must be lowercase, use only letters, numbers and hyphens (e.g. le-moulin-des-sources). Click Save — the new URL takes effect immediately.

If you change your slug, any existing links (Facebook bio, email signatures) will need updating.

Adding a hero / cover photo

Go to Settings → Booking page and upload a cover photo. This is the large banner image at the top of your booking page. Recommended size: 1400×600px minimum, landscape orientation.

Adding room photos

Go to Rooms, click a room to open it, then click Add photo. Photos appear on your booking page alongside room descriptions. Photo limits per plan:

  • Free — 1 photo per room
  • Pro — up to 5 photos per room
  • Multi — up to 10 photos per room

Good photos are the single biggest factor in booking conversion. Even one clear, well-lit photo per room makes a real difference.

Room descriptions and amenities

For each room, you can add a short description and tick amenities (WiFi, en-suite, balcony, garden view, etc.). These appear on your booking page to help guests choose. Go to Rooms → [Room name] → Edit.

Sharing your booking page

Your booking page URL is at Settings → Booking page → Share link. Common places to add it:

  • Instagram bio (one link, make it direct bookings)
  • Facebook page Book Now button (see section below)
  • Google Business Profile website link
  • Email signature
  • Booking.com review replies

Facebook Booking Button

Add a Book Now button to your Facebook business page

How to add the Book Now button

  1. First, copy your NestBook booking page URL from Settings → Booking page → Share link (e.g. nestbook.io/book/your-property).
  2. Go to your Facebook business page and click the blue + Add a Button button below your cover photo (or Edit button if one already exists).
  3. Select Book with you from the list of button types.
  4. Click Book Now and paste your NestBook booking page URL into the website field.
  5. Click Save. The button appears on your page immediately.

Any guest who finds you on Facebook can now book directly with you — no Booking.com commission.

Taking bookings

How the booking widget works and what guests see

Pro

How the booking widget works

The NestBook booking widget is available on Pro and Multi plans. Guests select dates, choose rooms, and complete a booking request — all without leaving your website.

You can embed the widget on any website by copying the script tag from Settings → Embed widget and pasting it into your HTML before the closing </body> tag. Or, direct guests straight to your NestBook booking page.

Booking requests vs confirmed bookings

Individual room (IP) mode: Guests complete a booking and it is confirmed immediately. Both you and the guest receive a confirmation email. The booking appears in your calendar right away.

Whole property (WP) mode: Guests submit a booking request. You receive an email and must Accept or Decline within 48 hours. The dates are held as pending during this time.

Whole property booking request flow

  1. Guest fills in dates, party size and contact details on your booking page.
  2. Guest submits the request — dates show as pending on your calendar.
  3. You receive an email notification with guest details and dates.
  4. You click Accept or Decline in the email (or from your dashboard).
  5. Guest receives a confirmation or decline email automatically.

Guest confirmation emails

When a booking is confirmed, the guest receives an email containing:

  • Booking reference number
  • Property name and address
  • Check-in and check-out dates and times
  • Room(s) booked and total price
  • Any special notes you have added

Access code and automated arrival emails

In Settings → Access & arrival (whole property mode), you can enter a key safe code, door code, or any arrival instructions. NestBook will automatically send this to your guest by email a set number of days before their arrival — so you don't have to remember to send it manually.

Deposit settings

In Settings → Deposits, you can specify a deposit amount or percentage that guests should pay to confirm their booking. This is shown to guests on the booking page. NestBook does not process payments directly — deposits are collected via your own payment method (bank transfer, PayPal, etc.).

Managing bookings

Calendar, booking details, check-in/out and cleaning status

The visual calendar

The Calendar page gives you a colour-coded weekly view of all rooms and bookings. Each booking shows the guest name and colour code by status. Hover or click any booking for details.

In whole property mode, the calendar shows a monthly view with one property — colour-coded by booking status (pending, confirmed, arrived, cleaning).

Clicking a booking to see details

Click any booking in the calendar or bookings list to open the booking detail panel. From here you can see guest contact info, dates, room(s), price, notes, and take actions.

Check in / Check out (IP mode)

In individual room mode, open a booking and use the Check in / Check out buttons to update the guest's status. The calendar updates immediately to reflect their arrival or departure.

Guests arrived / Guests departed (WP mode)

In whole property mode, confirmed bookings show Guests arrived and Guests departed actions. Marking guests as arrived changes the calendar colour. Marking as departed triggers the cleaning status.

Cleaning status (WP mode)

After guests depart, the property automatically shows a Needs cleaning status on the calendar. Once cleaning is complete, click Mark as clean — the property status updates to ready for the next guests.

Editing a booking

Open any booking and click Edit. You can change dates, rooms, guest details, notes, and price. Changes are saved immediately and do not send a new confirmation email to the guest unless you check the option to do so.

Adding notes

Open any booking and use the Notes field to add internal notes about the guest's preferences, special requests, arrival arrangements, and so on. Notes are private — guests never see them.

iCal sync

Sync with Booking.com, Airbnb and other platforms

What iCal sync does

iCal sync connects your NestBook calendar with external platforms (Booking.com, Airbnb, etc.). When a booking is made on any platform, it blocks those dates everywhere — preventing double bookings.

NestBook provides an iCal export URL (for platforms to read your availability) and can import external iCal feeds (to block dates from other platforms in NestBook).

Getting your iCal URL

Go to Settings → iCal sync. You'll see an iCal export URL for each room (IP mode) or for the whole property (WP mode). Copy the URL and paste it into the relevant platform.

In whole property mode there is one URL for the property. In individual room mode, each room has its own URL.

Adding to Booking.com

  1. In your Booking.com extranet, go to Calendar → iCal connections.
  2. Click Export calendar and copy the Booking.com iCal URL.
  3. Paste it into NestBook under Settings → iCal sync → Add external feed.
  4. Copy your NestBook iCal URL and paste it into Booking.com's Import calendar field.

Adding to Airbnb

  1. In Airbnb, go to your listing and open Availability → Sync calendars.
  2. Click Export Calendar and copy the Airbnb iCal URL.
  3. Paste it into NestBook under Settings → iCal sync → Add external feed.
  4. Copy your NestBook iCal URL and paste it into Airbnb's Import Calendar field.

Seasonal pricing

Set different rates for peak season, school holidays and quiet periods

Pro / Multi

Setting up rate periods

Go to Pricing → Rate periods. Click Add period and set a date range, a name (e.g. "High season", "Christmas") and a multiplier or flat price override. You can create as many periods as you need — they stack correctly, with specific date ranges taking priority over general ones.

Per-room price overrides

Each rate period can have a global multiplier (e.g. 1.5× = 50% more) or a per-room override. To set a per-room price, expand the rate period and enter a fixed nightly rate for each room. Leave blank to use the global multiplier.

How prices calculate night by night

When a guest selects dates that span multiple rate periods, NestBook calculates the correct price for each night individually — the total shown is always accurate. The breakdown is shown to guests on the booking page.

Room charges

Add extras, services and charges to any booking

Multi

Adding service categories

Go to Settings → Charges to set up your service categories — for example: Breakfast, Dinner, Bar, Spa, Laundry, Transfers. Each category appears as a tab when adding charges to a booking.

Adding charges to a booking

Open any booking and click Add charge. Select the category, enter a description, quantity and price. Charges are added to the booking total and appear on the guest's final bill. You can add multiple charges of different types.

Voiding charges

To remove a charge, open the booking, find the charge in the list, and click Void. The charge is struck through and excluded from the total — it remains visible in the record for audit purposes.

Reports

Revenue summaries, CSV export and expenses

Pro / Multi

Revenue reports

Go to Reports in the sidebar. You'll see a monthly revenue summary, occupancy rate, average booking value, and booking source breakdown. Use the date picker to view any period.

CSV export

On the Reports page, click Export CSV to download a spreadsheet of all bookings in the selected period. The CSV includes guest name, email, dates, rooms, price, status and booking source — useful for accounting or tax returns.

Expenses tracker

Go to Reports → Expenses to log property running costs — cleaning, maintenance, utilities, supplies. Add a category, description, date and amount. Expenses are shown on the Reports overview alongside revenue so you can see your net position at a glance.

Settings

Property details, staff, deposits, arrival instructions and more

Property details

Edit your property name, type, address, phone number and description. These appear on your booking page and in guest confirmation emails. Changes take effect immediately.

Breakfast hours

If you offer breakfast (B&B mode), set your breakfast serving hours in Settings → Property details. These appear on the booking page and in confirmation emails so guests know when to come down.

Deposit settings

Go to Settings → Deposits to set a deposit requirement. You can specify a fixed amount or a percentage of the total booking value. The deposit amount is shown to guests when they book, with instructions to pay via your chosen method.

Staff accounts and roles

Go to Settings → Staff to invite team members. You can assign roles:

  • Owner — full access to everything including billing and settings
  • Manager — can manage bookings, rooms, calendar and reports; cannot access billing
  • Staff — can view bookings and calendar; cannot edit settings or rates

Staff members receive an invitation email and create their own password.

Access & arrival (WP mode)

Go to Settings → Access & arrival to set up automated arrival emails. Enter your key safe code, parking instructions, WiFi password or any arrival notes. Choose how many days before arrival to send the email (typically 1–3 days).

NestBook sends the arrival email automatically — no need to remember to do it manually for each guest.

Error reporting

If you encounter a bug or unexpected behaviour, use the Report an issue link in the sidebar footer or email hello@nestbook.io. Please include your property name, a description of the issue, and any screenshots if possible.

Plans and billing

Free, Pro and Multi — what's included, how to upgrade

What's included in each plan

  • FreeFree — Public booking page, enquiry form for guests, 1 photo per room, iCal export, multi-language page
  • Pro£19/mo — Everything in Free, plus: full booking widget, online bookings, seasonal pricing, reports, CSV export, 5 photos per room, access & arrival emails, 30-day trial
  • Multi£38/mo — Everything in Pro, plus: room charges, expenses tracker, staff accounts with roles, 10 photos per room, priority support

See the full feature comparison →

How to upgrade

Go to Settings → Subscription in your dashboard and click Upgrade plan. Select Pro or Multi and complete the checkout. Your new plan is active immediately. The first 30 days are free — no charge until the trial ends.

How to cancel

Go to Settings → Subscription and click Cancel subscription. You'll keep full Pro or Multi access until the end of your current billing period. After that, your account reverts to the Free plan — your data and booking page are never deleted.

30-day free trial

When you upgrade to Pro or Multi for the first time, you get 30 days free — no credit card required upfront. After 30 days, billing starts automatically. You can cancel at any time before then with no charge.
